Shivaji College, Delhi was established by Hon'ble Dr Panjabrao Deshmukh, Union Minister for Agriculture in the year 1961. In the year 1961, the trusteeship of the institution was taken by the The Government of Delhi and it was relocated to Karampura later in the year 1976 the campus was shifted to Raja Garden. Shivaji College offers 20 undergraduate and 3 postgraduate courses in the field of Commerce, Science, and Humanities and apart from this it also offers an add-on course in German to the students.

Location:

Shivaji College, Delhi is located at Mahatma Gandhi Marg, Shivaji Enclave, Raja Garden in New Delhi. Shakurbasti Railway Station is around 4 km away from the college. Indira Gandhi International Airport is approximately 20 km away from the college and Rajouri Garden Metro Station is around 1 km away from the college. MayaPuri Depot is around 3 km away from the institute. Candidates can hire a taxi or take an auto-rickshaw to reach the institution.
